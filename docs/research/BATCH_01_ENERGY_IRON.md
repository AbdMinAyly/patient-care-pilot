## Scope

This register supports the connected pathway:

Fatigue → Iron deficiency → Anemia → Iron-rich diet → Iron supplement → Constipation → monitoring, tests, clinician questions, Your Plan, and Your SHINE Path.

The patient interface remains educational. It does not diagnose fatigue, prescribe an iron product, display dosing, or replace urgent assessment.

- Claims supported:

### NHLBI_IRON_DEFICIENCY_ANEMIA

- Organization: National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute
- Title: Iron-Deficiency Anemia
- Source URL: https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/health/anemia/iron-deficiency-anemia
- Page date noted: 2022-03-24
- Date checked: 2026-07-14
- Claims supported:
  - Iron deficiency can develop from blood loss, reduced absorption, increased needs, or inadequate intake.
  - Symptoms may include fatigue, dizziness, cold hands and feet, pale skin, breathlessness, or chest pain in more serious disease.
  - Food sources include beans, dried fruit, eggs, lean meat, fish, fortified cereals or breads, peas, tofu, and leafy vegetables.
  - Vitamin C–rich foods support iron absorption.
  - Evaluation commonly includes CBC, hemoglobin, iron measures, and ferritin.
  - Oral or intravenous iron may be used depending on the clinical situation.
- Pages affected: Iron deficiency, Anemia, Iron-rich diet, Iron supplement, Fatigue
- Safety notes: The interface does not reproduce numeric laboratory cutoffs or dosing.
- Review status: researched; treatment and urgent wording remain clinical-review-required
