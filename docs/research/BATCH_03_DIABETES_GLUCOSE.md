## Cross-check notes

- Diabetes symptoms were checked across NIDDK and CDC, and kept separate from diagnostic criteria.
- Common treatment targets were checked across NIDDK and CDC, then labeled as general references rather than personal prescriptions.
- Low-glucose symptoms, causes, oral safety, glucagon, and escalation were checked separately across NIDDK, CDC, and MedlinePlus.
- DKA mechanism and warning symptoms were checked across CDC and NIDDK; ketone and insulin-correction algorithms were not transferred.
- HbA1c interpretation was cross-checked against diagnostic, management, anemia, iron-deficiency, transfusion, kidney, pregnancy, and hemoglobin-variant limitations.
- Metformin high-risk warnings were checked against MedlinePlus, NIDDK, and FDA labeling.
- Insulin roles, hypoglycemia, storage, delivery, and infection-control warnings were checked across NIDDK, CDC, and MedlinePlus.
- Diet content was checked across NIDDK and CDC plate-method and meal-planning resources. No food is presented as a cure or replacement for medicine.
