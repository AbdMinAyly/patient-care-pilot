## Cross-check notes

- The Immunity teaching page was cross-checked across NIAID immune-system information, CDC prevention guidance, NIH ODS nutrition and supplement evidence, and FDA supplement safety.
- Cold and flu-like illness wording separates a symptom pattern from a confirmed diagnosis and uses CDC sources for symptoms, higher-risk assessment, urgent warning signs, spread reduction, and early clinician contact.
- Immune deficiency wording distinguishes primary genetic disorders from acquired immune suppression and avoids diagnosing from infection frequency alone.
- Autoimmune wording uses NIAID and MedlinePlus to separate immune overactivity from immune deficiency, explain variable organ involvement, and avoid interpreting one antibody result as proof.
- Cancer-treatment wording follows NCI guidance that infection during neutropenia can be life-threatening. The app deliberately uses the treatment team’s threshold rather than hard-coding a universal fever value.
- Malnutrition wording uses MedlinePlus for causes, risks, symptoms, screening, and referral while keeping refeeding and nutrition-support decisions under clinical review.
- Supplement wording uses NIH ODS and FDA to distinguish adequate intake from “immune boosting,” avoid product generalization, and emphasize interactions, overlapping ingredients, and adverse effects.
