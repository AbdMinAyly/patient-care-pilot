## Cross-check notes

- General adult blood-pressure categories were checked across NHLBI, AHA, CDC, and FDA. They are labeled as general office references, not individual treatment targets.
- The absence of symptoms was checked across NHLBI and CDC. Headache, fatigue, or dizziness is not used to prove or exclude hypertension.
- Home measurement technique was checked across CDC, NHLBI, and AHA. Device validation, cuff size, posture, rest, and a complete log are emphasized.
- DASH content was checked against NHLBI materials. Sodium numbers are retained only as general editorial references and are marked clinical-review-required.
- Potassium cautions were separated from routine food guidance because kidney disease and selected medicines can make supplements or salt substitutes unsafe.
- Medicine-class, adverse-effect, and interaction wording was checked across NHLBI, FDA, and MedlinePlus. No dose, titration, missed-dose, sick-day, or combination algorithm is displayed.
- Emergency symptoms and very high readings were checked across NHLBI, FDA, and AHA. Severe symptoms trigger emergency care; repeated measurement is not allowed to delay care.
- Pregnancy and postpartum warning signs were checked across NHLBI and ACOG and kept separate from routine adult categories.
