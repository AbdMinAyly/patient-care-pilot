## Scope

High cholesterol, LDL cholesterol, coronary and broader heart-disease education, Mediterranean-style eating, heart-health activity and lifestyle support, statins, monitoring, questions and tests to discuss, Your Plan, and Your SHINE Path. The pathway connects a silent lipid abnormality to overall cardiovascular risk without treating one laboratory value as a diagnosis or a complete treatment decision.

## Cross-check notes

- LDL and plaque wording was cross-checked across NHLBI and CDC. The patient interface states that high LDL can increase risk over time and usually causes no symptoms.
- The full lipid-profile approach was checked across NHLBI and CDC. LDL is not interpreted without HDL, triglycerides, prior results, and the clinical risk context.
- Familial hypercholesterolemia wording was checked against CDC. Numeric criteria, child pathways, genetic testing, and family cascade screening are withheld for clinical review.
- Heart-disease scope was checked across NHLBI and MedlinePlus. The page explains that heart disease is broad while focusing the cholesterol pathway on coronary heart disease.
- Mediterranean-style eating was checked against the American Heart Association. The pattern is presented flexibly and does not require one cuisine or alcohol.
- Activity wording was checked against ODPHP and AHA. General activity benefits are separated from heart-disease clearance, symptom stop rules, and cardiac rehabilitation.
- Statin benefit and adverse-effect wording was checked across MedlinePlus, CDC, and FDA. No product, dose, intensity, interaction adjustment, missed-dose, or stop/restart instruction appears.
- Pregnancy wording follows the FDA position and avoids the outdated claim that every pregnancy is an absolute contraindication. Most patients are advised to stop, while a small very-high-risk group requires specialist decisions.
- Chest and neurologic warning signs are separated from cholesterol testing. A normal or unknown LDL result does not reduce urgency when emergency symptoms are present.
