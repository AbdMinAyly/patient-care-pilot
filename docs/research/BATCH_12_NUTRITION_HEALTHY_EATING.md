## Cross-check notes

- General pattern wording was cross-checked across the current federal Dietary Guidelines, MyPlate, CDC healthy-eating guidance, and NIDDK practical healthy-living resources.
- Hydration wording was separated from oral rehydration and fluid-restriction care. The app deliberately avoids a universal water amount.
- Label wording uses FDA guidance to compare similar packaged foods while avoiding a single-food scoring system.
- Allergy wording uses FDA label guidance but does not provide diagnosis, reintroduction, challenge, or emergency treatment instructions.
- Budget and access language recognizes frozen, canned, shelf-stable, and repeat meals as legitimate tools rather than presenting fresh, scratch-cooked food as the only healthy option.
- Culture and dietary preferences are treated as design requirements for the plan, not barriers to be removed.
- Blood-sugar lifestyle wording was cross-checked with NIDDK and CDC and keeps carbohydrate prescriptions, medicine matching, personal thresholds, and emergency actions under clinical review.

The following remain `clinical-review-required`:

- Weight-loss, weight-gain, diabetes, kidney, liver, heart-failure, pregnancy, childhood, older-adult, eating-disorder, malnutrition, allergy, and swallowing adaptations.
- Insulin and medicine matching, personal glucose targets, low-glucose treatment, sick-day plans, fasting, ketone instructions, and emergency thresholds.

## Repetition and consistency review

- Openings are direct and describe one purpose at a time.
- Task verbs use Track, Record, Add, Review, Compare, Prepare, Ask, and Discuss.
- “Balanced” is presented as a flexible pattern, not a rigid plate prescription.
- “Water as default” is presented with fluid-restriction and illness cautions.
- Food swaps are gradual and do not classify foods as morally good or bad.
- No page claims that food alone diagnoses, cures, or replaces medical treatment.
- No dose, calorie level, fluid amount, carbohydrate amount, or emergency threshold is visible without clinical approval.
