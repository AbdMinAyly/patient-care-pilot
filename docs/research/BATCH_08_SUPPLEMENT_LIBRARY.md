## Cross-check notes

- Iron content was rechecked against NIH ODS, MedlinePlus, and FDA. The v030 page was expanded without changing its core rule that fatigue alone does not diagnose deficiency and that overdose prevention is essential.
- Vitamin B12 role, food sources, assessment, medicine contributors, and route options were checked across NIH ODS and MedlinePlus. The app distinguishes deficiency treatment from unsupported “energy booster” marketing.
- Vitamin D role, test selection, toxicity, and interactions were checked across NIH ODS and MedlinePlus. The app avoids universal testing, universal targets, or claims that vitamin D treats unrelated symptoms or chronic conditions.
- Oral-versus-injection wording was checked against FDA’s definition of dietary supplements and the NIH and MedlinePlus route descriptions for iron and B12. Injections and infusions are presented as prescription or clinical treatments.
- Side-effect and interaction content was cross-checked with FDA consumer-safety material and nutrient-specific sources. The app requires the exact product, route, ingredients, and medicine list rather than applying one general timing rule.
- Monitoring content links each supplement to a defined purpose, baseline, response, adverse effects, underlying cause, and a continue/change/stop decision. No test threshold or interval is shown.
