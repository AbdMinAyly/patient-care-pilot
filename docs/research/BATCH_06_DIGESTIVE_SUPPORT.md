## Cross-check notes

- Constipation definitions and warning signs were checked across multiple NIDDK pages. The app acknowledges individual bowel patterns and does not diagnose constipation from frequency alone.
- Fiber wording was checked across NIDDK and MedlinePlus. The app emphasizes gradual food-based increases and suitable fluids while withholding numeric targets and supplement dosing.
- Vomiting and diarrhea causes were checked across NIDDK and MedlinePlus. The app groups likely categories without claiming a specific diagnosis.
- Dehydration symptoms and fluid-replacement wording were checked across MedlinePlus, NIDDK, and CDC. The app distinguishes ordinary hydration from oral rehydration needs during meaningful fluid loss.
- Meal-tolerance wording follows NIDDK guidance that routine fasting and broad restrictive diets are generally unnecessary for acute diarrhea. The app does not prescribe a BRAT diet.
- Medicine contributors were cross-checked across constipation and diarrhea sources. The app instructs users to compile a complete list and seek pharmacist or clinician review rather than stopping treatment.
- Sick-day wording is deliberately non-prescriptive. Product-specific continuation, pause, restart, missed-dose, and monitoring instructions remain clinical-review-required.
- Diet Builder items distinguish gradual fiber-building meals from smaller meal-tolerance options. A bowel-support item is not presented as the correct choice during active diarrhea when fiber worsens symptoms.
